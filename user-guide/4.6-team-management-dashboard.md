# Section 4.6: Team Management Dashboard

The **Team Management** dashboard (found under **Team Management** in the producer sidebar, or at `/producer/team-management`) is the operational command center for managing team member scheduling, tracking utilization, resolving scheduling conflicts, managing your roster, and building staffing templates.

---

## 1. Roster Tab

The **Roster** tab (rendered via [RosterTable](https://github.com/cthomas32/abram-network/blob/main/src/components/team/RosterTable.tsx)) is the centralized directory for all personnel in your organization's network, managing both registered on-platform users and external contacts.

* ** Roster Directory**: Every contact displays their name, contact details, member type (Individual vs. Company), location, tags, capabilities, and billing rates (Hourly and Day rates).
* **Roster Management**: Managers can manually add external crew contacts, edit rates and primary roles inline, or delete contacts.
* **On-Platform Sync**: Displays the member status:
  * **On-Platform**: Connected to a registered user account (`user_id` is linked).
  * **External**: Unregistered contact with an email address. Managers can invite external crew to projects, prompting them to register on the platform.

---

## 2. Overview Tab

The **Overview** tab provides a real-time health check on your organization's staffing and workforce utilization:

* ** Roster Metrics**:
  * *Active Roster Size*: Total registered and unregistered individuals and companies in your network.
  * *Average Utilization*: Overall booked capacity percentage across all staff.
  * *Pending Invitations*: Total outstanding project invites awaiting crew RSVP.
  * *Schedule Conflicts*: Total unresolved double-bookings, overcommitments, or capacity overages.
* **Roster People Grid**: Displays each team member (filtered to people only) with their primary role, division, department, and location. It shows:
  * **Aggregate Weekly Booked Hours**: Combined hours from all active bookings.
  * **Aggregate Weekly Capacity**: Maximum weekly hours configured for the individual.
  * **Utilization Gauge**: A color-coded progress bar representing the member's aggregate utilization.
* **Utilization Details**: Clicking any row opens the [TeamMemberDetailModal](https://github.com/cthomas32/abram-network/blob/main/src/components/team/TeamMemberDetailModal.tsx) to inspect details, specific project assignments, and rates.

---

## 3. Calendar Tab

The **Calendar** tab renders a unified timeline mapping out the schedules of all personnel and resources:

* **Visual Timeline**: Uses [UnifiedScheduleCalendar](https://github.com/cthomas32/abram-network/blob/main/src/components/scheduling/UnifiedScheduleCalendar.tsx) to display every team member and resource as a row. Banners are color-coded based on booking categories (e.g., `project_work`, `time_off`, `meeting`, `personal`, `external_commitment`, `resource_booking`).
* **Folder Grouping**: Managers can organize the roster into folder groups (e.g., "Camera Department", "Grip & Electric") to filter the calendar view.
* **Drag-and-Drop Scheduling**: Block holds can be dragged to change start/end dates. If in a premium billing tier, the full drag-and-drop scheduler is enabled; otherwise, it operates in a read-only mode with upgrade triggers.
* **Direct Booking**: Double-clicking an empty slot on a team member's row opens the booking editor to create a schedule hold or associate them with a work package.
* **Resource and Kit Support**: Toggle **Show Kits** to display equipment allocations and unified gear kits directly alongside personnel on the calendar.

## 4. Capacity Planning Tool

The **Planning** tab (rendered via [CapacityPlanningTool](https://github.com/cthomas32/abram-network/blob/main/src/components/team/CapacityPlanningTool.tsx)) provides granular controls for analyzing and booking team hours:

### Capacity & Utilization Math

Capacity and utilization are calculated via Supabase RPCs ([get_team_member_utilization_batch](https://github.com/cthomas32/abram-network/blob/main/supabase/migrations/20260711000000_unified_scheduling_roles_and_locations.sql#L338) and [get_team_resource_overview](https://github.com/cthomas32/abram-network/blob/main/supabase/migrations/20260711000000_unified_scheduling_roles_and_locations.sql#L38)):

1. **Weekly Capacity**:
   * **People**: Check `contractor_profiles.total_weekly_hours` first. If null, fall back to the scheduling role default (`custom_roles.typical_hours_per_day * 5`). If no role is set, default to `40` hours.
   * **Resources**: Calculated as `custom_roles.typical_hours_per_day * 5 * organization_resources.quantity`, falling back to `40 * quantity` if no role is defined.
2. **Booked Hours**:
   * **People**: Sum of `hours_per_week` from bookings in `confirmed` or `active` status with category `project_work`, overlapping the target date range.
   * **Resources**: Sum of allocated hours based on clock duration: `(end_time - start_time) * 5 * quantity` from non-cancelled `project_resource_allocations` in the range.
3. **Availability Status**:
   * **Busy**: Utilization $\ge$ 100%.
   * **Limited**: Utilization $\ge$ 75% but < 100%.
   * **Available**: Utilization < 75%.

> [!NOTE]
> Utilization filters support three scopes: **All Utilization** (includes projects, meetings, personal, time-off), **Project Work Only**, and **Non-Project Only**.

## 5. Conflict Detection Panel

The **Conflicts** tab automatically flags scheduling issues across your entire roster:

### Overlap Detection Rules

Conflicts are identified using [detect_booking_conflicts](https://github.com/cthomas32/abram-network/blob/main/supabase/migrations/20260602000000_rebuild_conflict_detection_system.sql#L67):

* **Unavailable**: Flagged when a booking overlaps with a team member's `time_off` booking. (Severity: *Critical*)
* **Overcommitment**: Flagged when the sum of a member's planned weekly hours (timed clock-durations + spread weekday hours) exceeds their weekly capacity. (Severity: *Warning* if over capacity; *Critical* if exceeding capacity by > 20%)
* **Hard Clock-Time Conflict**: Flagged when two timed (non-all-day) bookings on the same day overlap in clock time. (Severity: *Critical*)
* **Back-to-Back Overload**: Flagged when a member has more than 8 hours of timed bookings scheduled on a single day. (Severity: *Warning*)
* **Resource Overallocation**: Triggered via `resource_conflicts_after` on `project_resource_allocations` when the total allocated quantity of a resource (e.g. cameras, studios) exceeds its maximum available quantity on a given day. (Severity: *Critical*)

### Conflict Resolution

Each conflict card details the overlap (severity, dates, conflicting projects) and provides a **Resolve** button to open the booking editor, reduce hours, change dates, or assign an alternative crew member.

## 6. Team Templates

The **Templates** tab (rendered via [TeamTemplateBuilder](https://github.com/cthomas32/abram-network/blob/main/src/components/organizations/TeamTemplateBuilder.tsx)) allows designing reusable staffing blueprints for standard project types (e.g., "Standard Commercial Shoot").

### Blueprint Structures

* **Template Structure**: Stored in `org_team_templates` (id, organization_id, name, description, category).
* **Role Slots**: Configured in `org_team_template_roles` (role name, typical rate, typical weekly hours, sort order, and optional default contractor user).
* **Import from Past Crew**: Allows creating a template instantly by copying the team structure of a previously completed project.
* **Instantiate Crew**: Applying a template to a project creates slots in `project_team_members`. When these roles are accepted by crew, the system triggers `update_project_team_invitation_status` to automatically generate `bookings` rows with `booking_source = 'ai_project'` and scheduled `hours_per_week` distributed across the project/work package duration.

## 7. Hours & Timesheet Verification

The **Hours** tab is the timesheet auditing center (rendered via [HoursRecordTable](https://github.com/cthomas32/abram-network/blob/main/src/components/team/HoursRecordTable.tsx)):

### Timesheet Auditing & Variance

Freelancers log hours into `time_entries`. The table aligns these entries with scheduled project bookings to calculate a comparison:

* **Logged Hours**: Actual hours worked submitted by the freelancer (`hours_worked`).
* **Planned (Recorded) Hours**: Daily expected hours based on bookings (`hours_per_week / 5` on weekdays, `0` on weekends).
* **Variance**: Calculated as `Logged Hours - Planned Hours`.
  * **Positive Variance**: Green badge (`+{hours}h`) showing extra hours worked over the plan.
  * **Negative Variance**: Amber badge (`-{hours}h`) showing fewer hours worked than planned.

### Administrative & Non-Project Entries

* **Nullable Projects**: Time entries support nullable `project_id` and custom categories (`project_work`, `admin_time`, `time_off`), enabling team members to log administrative overhead or internal meetings.
* **Work Order Completion Auto-population**: When a Work Order status is changed to `wrapped` or `completed` by a manager, the database function `cascade_work_order_status` automatically populates the `time_entries` table for all assigned personnel. It calculates daily hours from booking durations and resolves hourly rates using organization memberships or contractor profiles.
* **Actual Cost Rollup**: Any manual or auto-populated entry in `time_entries` or `expenses` triggers `sync_work_package_actual_costs()`, rolling up costs to update the parent work package's actual spend in real time.
* **Approval Flow**: Managers verify, edit, or delete logged hours. Approved hours sync directly to the billing ledger to execute payouts.
