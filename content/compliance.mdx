## 🗺️ Work Order Status Cascade

In ABRAM, a **Work Order** serves as the contract and scheduling agreement between the producer and the freelancer (or equipment kit). The database coordinates the booking status of all assigned personnel and resources as the work order progresses:

| Work Order Status | Linked Resource Booking Status | Crew Booking Status | Operational Behavior |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Draft** / **On Hold** | `reserved` | Hold | Resources are blocked out; crew calendars show tentative holds. |
| **Confirmed** / **Pending** | `reserved` | Pending Invite / Hold | Roster invitations are sent; gear remains blocked out. |
| **In Progress** | `in_use` | Active | Crew is actively checked in; resources are marked in-use. |
| **Wrapped** / **Completed** | `returned` | Completed | Crew completed assignment; gear is released back to inventory. |
| **Cancelled** | `cancelled` | Cancelled | Gear and calendar hours are freed up immediately. |

## 1. Structure of a Work Order Agreement

Work orders are created within active Work Packages and contain the following legal and operational terms:
* **Scope & Guidelines**: Custom text field specifying the shoot guidelines, directions, deliverables, and safety requirements.
* **Timeline**: Exact start and end dates and times, supporting hourly or all-day events.
* **Location**: Specific physical address or remote URL workspace link.
* **Personnel & Resource Rates**: Standard hourly/day rates for crew and rental rates for equipment.

## 2. Real-Time Conflict Checking & Database Rules

To prevent operational double-bookings, ABRAM runs conflict validation rules:

### A. Personnel Conflicts (Soft Warnings)
* **Soft Warning System**: When a producer assigns a freelancer to a work order, the system queries the network schedule.
* **Informational Alerts**: If the freelancer has overlapping confirmed bookings or Google/Outlook calendar blockouts, a yellow **Conflict Warning** icon appears.
* **Permitted Action**: The database allows you to save the work order. This warning is purely informational, allowing producers to negotiate schedule details.

### B. Equipment & Resource Conflicts (Hard Blocks)
* **Hard Database Block**: Physical resources (cameras, gear, rooms, vehicles, or kits) are protected by strict database rules.
* **Double-Booking Prevention**: If you attempt to save or confirm a work order allocating a resource whose available inventory count is fully booked elsewhere during that timeframe, the database actively throws a validation error:
  `Insufficient resource availability for "[Resource Name]" during requested dates.`
* **Permitted Action**: The database **blocks the save operation**. You cannot save the work order until you resolve the resource conflict.

## 3. Kit Expansion & Group Rescheduling

Operational equipment is often bundled into packages (e.g., a "DSMC2 Camera Kit" containing a body, lenses, cards, and batteries).

* **Kit Expansion**: When a kit is added to a work order, the system expands the kit into its individual constituent items in the database.
* **Unified Group Syncing**: All individual parts of the kit remain linked. If you reschedule the work order dates or delete the booking, the database automatically updates or deletes all constituent items as a unified group, preventing kits from being fragmented.
* **Location Auto-Sync**: If you allocate a physical room or studio resource (e.g., "Stage 2") to a work order, the platform automatically syncs that room's physical address to the work order's main **Location** field.

## 4. Compliance Onboarding & Security Controls

Every user must complete compliance checks during account registration:

### Onboarding Steps
* **Structure Selection**: Users choose between Independent and Organization structures, which determines tax and invoicing setups.
* **Terms Consent**: Users must review and agree to the platform's **Terms of Use** and **Privacy Policy** to activate their dashboard.
* **AI Feature Consent (Optional)**: Users can optionally choose whether to allow ABRAM to use their workspace data (such as project schedules and descriptions) to train and refine platform AI assistance models.

### Profile Visibility Overrides
Freelancers can override profile settings in their **Profile Settings**:
* **Show on Company Profile**: Toggle whether to appear in the organization's member directory.
* **Allow Public Profile**: Toggle whether to publish an individual profile page in the external marketplace.
